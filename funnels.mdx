Funnels show you how many visitors progress through an ordered journey and — more importantly — where they stop. Each step in a funnel can match a URL, a custom event, or an existing goal definition, so you can model the real path your customers take from first interest to completed action. The output of a funnel is not a verdict on why visitors dropped off; it is a precise pointer to where you should investigate next.

## Plan the journey first

Before you open the funnel builder, write down the shortest sequence of steps that actually answers your question. Resist adding intermediate interactions that feel meaningful but do not represent a real decision point.

Here is a well-scoped example:

1. **Pricing viewed** — URL `/pricing`
2. **Checkout started** — event `checkout_started`
3. **Purchase completed** — goal `Purchase completed`

Three steps. Each step represents a meaningful stage gate. Adding a step for "clicked the pricing CTA" between steps 1 and 2 might seem useful, but it makes the funnel harder to read without improving your ability to act on the data.

<Tip>
  Aim for two to five steps per funnel. If you need more than ten steps, split the journey into two funnels — one for acquisition and one for activation, for example.
</Tip>

## Create a funnel

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the funnel builder">
    Open the website in MetricPanel, choose **Goals**, then scroll to the **Funnels** section and select **Create Funnel**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the journey">
    Give the funnel a name that describes the business question, such as "Pricing to purchase" or "Trial to paid conversion". Add an optional description for teammates.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add steps">
    Add between two and ten ordered steps. For each step:
    - Choose **URL** and provide a URL pattern
    - Choose **Event** and provide an exact event key
    - Choose **Goal** and select an existing goal definition
  </Step>
  <Step title="Order the steps">
    Reorder the steps to match the real customer journey. MetricPanel evaluates completion in the order you define, so step sequence matters.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and review">
    Save the funnel. MetricPanel immediately applies it to historical data within your current reporting window.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  URL steps accept a URL pattern with `*` wildcards. Event steps require an **exact** event key. Goal steps reference an existing goal definition — make sure the goal is active before depending on it in a funnel.
</Note>

## Read the result

For each step, compare the number of visitors who reached it with the number who reached the previous step. The percentage shown is the step-level conversion rate.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Large drop between steps" icon="arrow-trend-down">
    A big fall-off identifies where to investigate, but it does not prove why visitors stopped. Pair the funnel with source, device, page, and event context before making product changes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Consistent date ranges" icon="calendar">
    Use the same reporting date range when comparing funnel snapshots over time. A visitor must have enough time inside that range to complete the entire journey.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Make funnels reliable

Funnel data is only as trustworthy as the underlying instrumentation. Follow these practices to keep your funnels accurate:

- Use **one business meaning per event name** — never reuse `button_clicked` across unrelated actions.
- Keep **success routes inaccessible** until the action is genuinely complete, so pageview-based steps cannot fire prematurely.
- Prefer **explicit events** for actions that happen without a page navigation, such as modal submissions or in-page upgrades.
- Keep **goal definitions active and stable** while a funnel depends on them — pausing or deleting a goal removes it from funnel matching going forward.
- Test the **happy path** in a new browser session after changing instrumentation so you confirm end-to-end tracking before relying on new data.

<Warning>
  If a funnel step shows zero visitors, verify the underlying event or goal before drawing conclusions. Check your event stream in [Events](/events) and confirm the goal is active in [Goals](/goals).
</Warning>
