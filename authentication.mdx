MetricPanel's dashboard supports email and password sign-in, optional social sign-in via Google or GitHub, email verification, password reset, and two-factor authentication (2FA). This page walks you through each flow so you can access your account and keep it secure.

## Sign Up and Sign In

Navigate to the MetricPanel dashboard to get started.

- **Sign up** — go to `/signup` and enter your email address and a password. You'll receive a verification email with a six-digit code to confirm your address.
- **Sign in** — go to `/login` and enter your email and password.
- **Social sign-in** — if your MetricPanel environment has Google or GitHub sign-in enabled, you'll see those buttons on the sign-in and sign-up pages. Click the provider button and authorize the connection to sign in without a password.

<Note>
  Social sign-in buttons only appear when the corresponding provider is configured for your MetricPanel deployment. If you don't see a Google or GitHub button, your environment uses email/password sign-in only.
</Note>

## Verify Your Email

After signing up with an email and password, MetricPanel sends a six-digit verification code to your inbox.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check your inbox">
    Look for an email from MetricPanel containing a six-digit verification code. The code expires after **15 minutes**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the code">
    Go to `/verify-email` and enter the six-digit code. You have **three attempts** before the code is invalidated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resend the code if needed">
    If the code expired or didn't arrive, use the resend link on the verification page to request a new code.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  If the verification page shows a message that email delivery is not available in your environment, resending will not work. Contact your administrator or the operator of your MetricPanel instance for assistance.
</Warning>

The verification page clearly distinguishes between an **invalid or expired code**, a **working resend path**, and an environment where **email delivery is not configured**. If you see the last message, don't keep retrying — reach out to your operator.

## Reset Your Password

If you've forgotten your password, use the forgot-password flow to set a new one.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to the forgot-password page">
    Click **Forgot password?** on the sign-in page, or navigate directly to `/forgot-password`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your email address">
    Type the email address associated with your MetricPanel account and submit the form.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check your inbox for the reset link">
    MetricPanel sends a password-reset link to your email address. Open the email and click the link.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a new password">
    On the `/reset-password` page, enter and confirm your new password, then submit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Sign in with your new password">
    Return to `/login` and sign in with your updated credentials.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  The password-reset email is only sent when outbound email delivery is configured for your MetricPanel deployment. If the forgot-password page shows a message that email delivery is unavailable, contact your administrator — the form will not silently pretend to send an email it cannot deliver.
</Note>

## Set Up Two-Factor Authentication

Protect your account with a time-based one-time password (TOTP) using an authenticator app.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the two-factor setup page">
    Navigate to `/two-factor` from your account settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scan the QR code">
    Open your authenticator app (such as Google Authenticator, Authy, or 1Password) and scan the QR code displayed on screen. Alternatively, enter the setup key manually if your app supports it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the verification code">
    Type the six-digit code currently shown in your authenticator app and confirm the setup. This verifies that your app is correctly synced.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your backup codes">
    MetricPanel displays one-time backup codes after 2FA is enabled. Save these somewhere secure — you'll need them if you lose access to your authenticator app.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Use a cloud-backed authenticator app so you don't lose access if you change devices. Store backup codes in a password manager rather than a plain text file.
</Tip>

## If Email Delivery Is Unavailable

Some MetricPanel deployments are configured without outbound email. In that case:

- The **password-reset flow** will display a message explaining that the reset email cannot be sent, rather than silently failing.
- The **email verification resend** option will not be available — the page will tell you clearly that email delivery is not configured.
- **Team invitations** sent to new members may not arrive — confirm directly with invitees rather than assuming delivery.

If you encounter any of these messages, contact your MetricPanel administrator or the operator of your deployment for assistance accessing your account.
