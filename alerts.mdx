Alerts watch a named metric and fire a notification the moment it crosses a threshold you define. Instead of checking dashboards manually, you can let MetricPanel notify your team over Slack, email, or a webhook when revenue spikes, traffic drops, a goal rate falls, or an error count climbs. A well-configured alert gives your team the time to investigate and respond before a problem compounds.

## Available rule fields

Each alert rule is built from the following fields:

| Field | Choices or meaning |
| --------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Type | Revenue, CAC, traffic, goal, or error |
| Metric | The metric key evaluated by the rule |
| Operator | `>`, `>=`, `<`, `<=`, or `=` |
| Threshold | Positive numeric value used by the comparison |
| Cooldown period | Minimum minutes between repeated notifications |
| Channel | Slack, email, or webhook |
| Destination | Channel-specific email address or URL, unless a website default exists |

<Info>
  Website owners and admins can create and manage alerts. Viewers can inspect alerts they have been granted access to, but cannot create or change rules.
</Info>

## Create an alert

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Alert settings">
    Open the website's **Settings**, then choose **Alerts** from the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Start a new rule">
    Select **Create Alert** to open the rule builder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name the rule">
    Name the rule after the condition **and** the expected response — for example, *"Revenue spike review"* or *"Checkout goal rate drop — investigate"*. A descriptive name helps teammates act quickly when a notification arrives.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure the condition">
    Choose the **type**, **metric**, **operator**, **threshold**, and **cooldown period**. Make sure the threshold units match the metric — use smallest currency units for revenue metrics.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a delivery channel">
    Select **Slack**, **email**, or **webhook**, then confirm its destination. See delivery requirements below if a channel is unavailable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and test">
    Save the rule, then immediately click **Test** to send a sample delivery to the destination. Do not depend on an alert rule until a test delivery confirms the destination is reachable.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Delivery requirements

Each channel has a prerequisite before it can receive notifications:

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Slack" icon="slack">
    Requires either a destination webhook URL entered on the rule itself, or a configured website-level Slack default in your MetricPanel environment.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Email" icon="envelope">
    Available only when the MetricPanel environment has email delivery configured. If email is unavailable in your environment, choose Slack or webhook instead.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Webhook" icon="webhook">
    Requires a destination URL that can accept an outbound POST request from MetricPanel. Confirm the endpoint is publicly reachable and returns a `2xx` response.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  An alert can only be saved when its selected channel is available in your environment. If email is not available, do **not** assume a message was sent — switch to Slack or webhook to ensure delivery.
</Warning>

## Test and review history

Testing an alert proves that the **current destination** can receive a sample delivery. It does not prove that the metric condition will ever trigger.

After the first real trigger fires, open the alert history and verify:

- The **evaluated value** that triggered the rule
- The **threshold** it was compared against
- The **channel** and **send state** (delivered, failed, or pending)
- Any **delivery error** reported for that attempt

Use the **cooldown period** to prevent a flood of repeated notifications during a sustained condition — for example, set a 60-minute cooldown if a sustained revenue spike should notify your team no more than once per hour.

<Tip>
  Pause alert rules during planned traffic tests, load tests, or billing sandbox activity. Notifications fired during these periods are misleading and can train your team to ignore future alerts.
</Tip>

## Operational checklist

Before relying on an alert rule in production, confirm each of the following:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Metric is populated" icon="chart-line">
    The metric key is already populated by the website's current instrumentation. An alert on an empty metric will never fire.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Threshold units are correct" icon="ruler">
    Threshold units match the metric — especially for revenue metrics that use smallest currency units such as cents.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Destination is monitored" icon="bell">
    The destination belongs to a monitored team channel or inbox — not a personal address that goes unread on weekends.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Test delivery succeeded" icon="circle-check">
    A test delivery succeeded before the rule went live. Re-test whenever you update the destination.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Someone owns the response" icon="user">
    A named person or on-call rotation knows what to do when a real notification arrives. An unowned alert is the same as no alert.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>
